Questions

Frequently Asked Questions

How do I know if my tree needs pruning?

Key signs that pruning may be needed include: crossing or rubbing branches, deadwood in the canopy, excessive shading on structures below, branches with narrow crotch angles (which are prone to failure), and an overall unbalanced or crowded crown. We recommend a professional assessment to determine the appropriate pruning type and timing for your species — not all trees benefit from pruning at the same time of year.

What is a Tree Risk Assessment?

A Tree Risk Assessment is a structured evaluation of a tree's likelihood of failure and the potential consequences of that failure. We use the TRAQ (Tree Risk Assessment Qualification) methodology — the ISA standard — to assess structural defects, root health, site conditions, and targets. The result is a written report with a risk rating and specific mitigation recommendations, suitable for insurance, legal, or property management purposes.

Are you ISA Certified?

Yes. Our lead arborist Edgar Guerrero holds an active ISA Certified Arborist credential, which requires passing a comprehensive examination, demonstrating significant field experience, and completing ongoing continuing education. We also hold the ISA Tree Risk Assessment Qualification (TRAQ). You can verify credentials at the ISA website using our certification number. We're happy to provide documentation on request.

Do you serve my area?

We serve clients throughout Sonoma County, Napa County, and Marin County in California — the North Bay region. This includes cities such as Santa Rosa, Petaluma, Sebastopol, Napa, St. Helena, San Rafael, Mill Valley, and surrounding communities. If you are unsure whether your property falls within our service area, please contact us and we will be glad to confirm.

What is Plant Health Care?

Plant Health Care (PHC) is a proactive, integrated approach to maintaining the health and vitality of trees and woody plants. Rather than reacting to problems as they appear, PHC involves regular monitoring, soil analysis, fertilization, and targeted treatments to prevent pest and disease issues before they become serious. We offer both organic and conventional treatment options, always prioritizing minimal environmental impact while maximizing tree health outcomes.

How do I prepare for tree removal?

Prior to removal, we recommend: confirming whether a permit is required from your local municipality (we can assist with this process), clearing the access path to the tree and surrounding area of vehicles, furniture, and obstacles, notifying adjacent neighbors, and making arrangements if pets or children need to be kept away from the work area during the day. We will walk you through the process in detail during our initial consultation and provide a clear timeline for the work.
