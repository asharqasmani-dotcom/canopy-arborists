Service 01

Tree Management Plans

A Tree Management Plan is the foundation of long-term tree care. Rather than reacting to problems as they arise, a management plan gives you a clear, prioritized roadmap for maintaining the health, safety, and value of every tree on your property. This service is particularly valuable for HOAs, commercial properties, municipalities, and estates with multiple trees or significant specimens.

We begin with a comprehensive site assessment — inventorying all trees, evaluating their condition, identifying any immediate hazards, and documenting baseline data. From this foundation, we build a multi-year care schedule that prioritizes work intelligently, helping you manage budgets and resources while maintaining a healthy tree canopy over time.

What’s Included
  • Full property tree inventory and condition assessment
  • Priority ranking of care and maintenance needs
  • Multi-year care schedule and budget planning
  • Written documentation and digital records
  • Species identification and health notes
  • Annual review and plan updates

Service 02

Tree Pruning & Structural Training

Pruning is the most commonly performed arboricultural practice — and one of the most commonly performed incorrectly. At Canopy Arborists, every pruning cut is made with a clear purpose: improving tree structure, removing hazards, or maintaining the health and aesthetics of the canopy. We follow ISA pruning standards and never recommend unnecessary or harmful practices such as topping.

For young trees, structural training is one of the most impactful investments you can make. Establishing good branch architecture early reduces the likelihood of structural failures and costly interventions later in the tree’s life. We work with your trees’ natural growth habits to develop strong, well-spaced scaffolding branches from the ground up.

What’s Included
  • Crown cleaning — deadwood, crossed and rubbing branch removal
  • Crown thinning for light and air penetration
  • Crown raising for clearance over structures and walkways
  • Crown reduction where appropriate and ISA-compliant
  • Structural training and formative pruning for young trees
  • Vista pruning for views and sight lines

Service 03

Plant Health Care Programs

Plant Health Care (PHC) is a proactive, integrated approach to maintaining the vitality of trees and woody plants. Rather than responding to visible problems after they become serious, PHC involves regular monitoring, soil analysis, targeted nutrition, and early intervention — keeping your trees healthy before issues escalate into costly treatments or losses.

Trees in urban and suburban environments face unique stresses: compacted soils, restricted root zones, pollution, pest pressure, and competition for resources. Our PHC programs are tailored to the specific conditions of your site and the species present. We offer both organic and conventional treatment options, always prioritizing the least invasive approach that will achieve the desired outcome.

What’s Included
  • Soil analysis and nutrient assessment
  • Targeted fertilization and soil amendment programs
  • Pest and disease monitoring and diagnosis
  • Organic and low-impact treatment options
  • Foliar and systemic treatment applications
  • Ongoing monitoring and program adjustments

Service 04

Tree Risk Assessment

A Tree Risk Assessment is a structured, professional evaluation of a tree’s likelihood of failure and the potential consequences of that failure. We use the TRAQ methodology — the ISA’s internationally recognized Tree Risk Assessment Qualification standard — to provide evaluations that are scientifically grounded, defensible, and clearly communicated.

Risk assessments are appropriate whenever you have concerns about a tree’s structural integrity, after storm damage, before purchasing a property, for insurance or legal documentation, or as part of a regular management program. Our written reports include a clear risk rating, the specific defects identified, and actionable mitigation recommendations.

What’s Included
  • TRAQ-compliant visual tree inspection
  • Advanced diagnostic techniques where warranted
  • Assessment of tree structure, root zone, and site conditions
  • Target analysis (what is at risk if the tree fails)
  • Written risk report with assigned risk rating
  • Specific mitigation recommendations and options

Root Zone & Soil Management

The health of a tree begins underground. Root zone and soil management addresses the conditions beneath the surface that ultimately determine whether a tree thrives or declines. Urban soils are frequently compacted, depleted of nutrients, and poorly structured — conditions that limit root development, restrict water and air infiltration, and stress trees over time.

We offer a range of soil and root zone services designed to improve growing conditions and address specific problems such as soil compaction, grade changes, root collar issues, and construction damage. These services are particularly valuable for established specimen trees, trees in high-traffic areas, and trees showing signs of decline with no obvious above-ground cause.

What’s Included
  • Soil aeration (vertical mulching, air spade)
  • Organic mulch application and management
  • Root collar excavation and basal inspection
  • Soil compaction remediation
  • Root pruning for infrastructure or construction
  • Post-construction root zone rehabilitation

Common Questions

Services FAQ

How do I know which service I need?

The best starting point is a consultation. We will assess your trees, listen to your concerns, and recommend only the services that are genuinely warranted — nothing more. If you have a specific concern (a leaning tree, dead branches, declining health, a recent storm), describe it when you contact us and we can advise you on the most appropriate next step.

Do I need a permit to remove a tree?

This depends on your municipality, the species of tree, its size, and its location on your property. Many cities and counties in Sonoma, Napa, and Marin have protected tree ordinances that require a permit before certain trees can be removed. We are familiar with local regulations across our service area and can advise you on permit requirements as part of our consultation — and assist with the application process where needed.

How long does a Tree Risk Assessment take?

A single-tree TRAQ assessment typically takes 45 minutes to 1.5 hours on-site, depending on access and the complexity of the tree. The written report is typically delivered within 3–5 business days. Assessments for multiple trees or larger properties will require more time — we will provide an accurate estimate when you contact us with the details of your site.

What is the best time of year for tree pruning?

The optimal pruning time depends on the species and the pruning objective. In general, dormant-season pruning (late fall through early spring) is appropriate for many deciduous trees. However, oaks in California should not be pruned during the oak wilt transmission season (April–July) to avoid vector-transmitted pathogens. Certain flowering trees are best pruned immediately after bloom. We assess the appropriate timing for your specific trees during the consultation.

Can you help with trees affected by fire or drought?

Yes. Fire and drought stress are significant concerns throughout the North Bay. Trees damaged by fire may appear dead but retain the ability to recover — assessment is essential before removal decisions are made. Drought-stressed trees often show delayed symptoms and benefit from soil management, targeted irrigation guidance, and PHC programs. We have experience working with fire-affected properties and can provide post-fire assessments and recovery recommendations.

Do you provide written reports for all services?

Written reports are standard for Tree Risk Assessments, Tree Management Plans, consulting engagements, and emergency damage documentation. For routine services such as pruning or plant health care, we provide written service records and can include notes on observed conditions. If you require formal documentation for legal, insurance, or property transaction purposes, please let us know when you contact us so we can ensure the appropriate report format is prepared.
